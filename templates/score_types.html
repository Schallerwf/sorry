Ultimate Sorry uses two different ways to determine the 'score' for a player.

1. Distance
The distance score works how you might expect. A player's score is the sum of each of it's pawns distance towards home. Pawns in 'Start' are worth zero points. Pawns in 'Home' are worth 66. All spots in between are incremental. See the board below for the distance score of each position for the Yellow player.

1. rfScore
rfScore is short for 'Random Forest Score'. A random forest is a simple machine learning alrgorithm that has looked at hundreds of thousands of Sorry games and attempts to predict which player will win. Each player is given a score between 0.0 and 1.0, where the closer to 1.0 the more likely the player will win. This algorithm will sometimes identify moves that are create a better board for a player even if the distance is not optimal.
